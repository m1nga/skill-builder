# Foundation Patterns

Use only the pattern required by the diagnosis. Names are examples, not required filenames.

## Contents

1. Recovery packet
2. Purpose clarification
3. Repair map
4. Clean rebuild bundle
5. Continuation probe
6. Hold record
7. Minimal acceptance evidence

## 1. Recovery packet

Use when source material was lost or damaged.

- recovery-source inventory;
- exact/partial/reconstructed/missing classification;
- provenance per recovered artifact;
- hashes or immutable copies where appropriate;
- unresolved gaps;
- restoration target that does not overwrite evidence.

Do not call a conceptual rewrite a recovery.

## 2. Purpose clarification

Use when the project cannot yet govern trade-offs.

- current problem and affected actor;
- intended change;
- why the product should exist;
- confirmed decisions with primary anchors;
- hypotheses and open questions;
- explicit non-goals;
- cheapest disconfirming probe.

The output may be a conversation synthesis, decision note, or compact mandate. It need not be a
large product document.

## 3. Repair map

Use when the direction remains valid.

- one active foundation;
- contradiction/dependency map;
- `KEEPS`, `REPLACES`, and `REMOVES`;
- affected files, code, tests, prompts, data, and interfaces;
- acceptance checks;
- zero-context answer expected after repair.

Repair dependent surfaces together; do not leave warning labels beside stale truth.

## 4. Clean rebuild bundle

Use when old context cannot safely author the new foundation.

- user-current evidence with origin and date;
- observed reality and verified mechanisms;
- AI proposals with proposer and status;
- portable lessons only;
- source manifest with included/excluded reasons;
- unknowns and do-not-invent list.

A fresh builder reads only this bundle. The history-reading planner does not certify the result.

## 5. Continuation probe

Use when another rebuild would avoid reality.

- one user-visible or operational outcome;
- current hypothesis;
- minimal vertical path;
- real external sensor;
- pass/fail or learning evidence;
- fixed time/cost boundary;
- decision changed by the result.

Internal tests alone do not prove customer value.

## 6. Hold record

Use when construction should stop.

- what survives and where;
- why work is paused or retired;
- what evidence would justify reopening;
- unresolved risks;
- recovery and deletion boundaries.

Holding a project is a valid diagnosis, not failure to complete a rebuild.

## 7. Minimal acceptance evidence

Select evidence proportional to the intervention:

- exact-source provenance for recovery;
- user-confirmed purpose distinctions for clarification;
- contradiction scan and affected tests for repair;
- independent zero-context probe for rebuild;
- external outcome receipt for continuation;
- verified preservation and stop condition for hold.

The acceptance evidence should prove the limiting condition changed. More documents do not.
